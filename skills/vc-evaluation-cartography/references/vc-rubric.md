# VC Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric to manually review and, when justified by evidence, adjust the baseline score generated by `scripts/score.py`. Score evidence quality, not storytelling polish.

## A. Market Size & Timing - 15 pts

- 13-15: Clear wedge into a large or fast-growing market, credible TAM/SAM/SOM, strong timing catalyst, and explicit beachhead.
- 9-12: Large market is plausible, but sizing or timing evidence is incomplete.
- 5-8: Market exists but wedge, urgency, or expansion path is weak.
- 0-4: Market is vague, too small, or unsupported.

## B. Problem Urgency & Customer Pain - 10 pts

- 8-10: Specific ICP, severe pain, measurable current cost, and direct customer discovery evidence.
- 5-7: Problem is credible but urgency or buyer detail is thin.
- 2-4: Broad pain statement with little customer proof.
- 0-1: Problem is unclear or solution-led.

## C. Product, Technology & Roadmap - 10 pts

- 8-10: Product is demonstrable, differentiated, technically feasible, and roadmap is milestone-based.
- 5-7: Product direction is clear but proof, integration detail, or roadmap is incomplete.
- 2-4: Conceptual product with limited evidence.
- 0-1: Product scope is unclear.

## D. Traction & Customer Evidence - 15 pts

- 13-15: Strong growth, paid customers, retention/cohort evidence, pipeline quality, and clear expansion signals.
- 9-12: Early traction exists but retention, repeatability, or pipeline quality needs validation.
- 5-8: Pilots, LOIs, waitlist, or usage exist but commercial evidence is limited.
- 0-4: No meaningful customer evidence.

## E. Business Model & Unit Economics - 10 pts

- 8-10: Pricing, ACV, gross margin, CAC/LTV/payback, and expansion model are credible.
- 5-7: Revenue model exists but unit economics are partial.
- 2-4: Pricing hypothesis without operating evidence.
- 0-1: Business model is unclear.

## F. Go-to-Market & Distribution - 10 pts

- 8-10: Repeatable motion, channel strategy, funnel metrics, sales cycle, and distribution advantage are clear.
- 5-7: GTM motion is plausible but not yet repeatable.
- 2-4: Generic sales/marketing plan.
- 0-1: No credible GTM path.

## G. Team & Founder-Market Fit - 10 pts

- 8-10: Team has strong domain expertise, execution record, recruiting plan, and founder-market fit.
- 5-7: Team is credible but has clear gaps.
- 2-4: Limited relevant experience or missing critical functions.
- 0-1: Team evidence is absent.

## H. Financial Plan & Fundraising Fit - 10 pts

- 8-10: Burn, runway, round size, valuation context, use of funds, and next-round milestones align.
- 5-7: Financing plan is understandable but assumptions need testing.
- 2-4: Basic raise ask without milestone logic.
- 0-1: No fundraising or financial plan.

## I. Moat, Competition & Defensibility - 5 pts

- 4-5: Competition is mapped and defensibility is evidence-backed through data, workflow lock-in, network, brand, regulatory, or technical advantage.
- 2-3: Competitive view exists but moat is mostly asserted.
- 0-1: Competition or defensibility is missing.

## J. Key Risks & Diligence Gaps - 5 pts

- 4-5: Major risks are named, mitigations are concrete, and diligence gaps are explicit.
- 2-3: Some risks are listed but not prioritized.
- 0-1: Risks are absent or superficial.

## Diligence Action Framing

- Effort: S (<2h), M (0.5-1 day), L (2+ days)
- Impact: Tie to a decision gate, such as "validates retention quality before partner meeting" or "tests whether valuation is supportable".
- Priority: `impact_score / effort_hours`; higher numbers come first.
